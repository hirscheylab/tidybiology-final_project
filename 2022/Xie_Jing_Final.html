#45 mice were randomly divided into 3 groups (15/group), receiving 1mg or 10mg bromocriptine or sham treatment (control, CTRL), the plasma levels of prolactin (PRL) were measured every 30 days from Day 0 to Dat 150 to relect the prolactinoma growth over time

#noticed that $ Mouse <int>, mice were numbered from 1 to 45 in this study, however, these numbers should be factors, if not converted to factors, will affect the plots
a$Mouse<-as.factor(a$Mouse)
a$Group = factor(a$Group,levels = c('CTRL','Bromo1','Bromo10'))
#also I want to make the control group (CTRL) as the reference in the box plots that I am going to make, thus I rearranged the order of the levels in the Group, otherwise CTRL would be placed after the two treatment groups at the far right of the figure

Final graph

The boxplots above showed huge differences over time between the groups taking different treatment, however, they did not reflect the changes of PRL of individuals over time. To visualize the changes of PRL of each mouse during the 150-day period, I made the following graph

p3<-ggplot(a,aes(Time,PRL,group=Mouse,color=Group))
p3+geom_point(size=1)+geom_smooth(method=lm,se=FALSE,size=0.5)+labs(title="PRL changes during the 150-day period")+theme(plot.title=element_text(color="blue",size=20,face="bold",hjust=0.5))
## `geom_smooth()` using formula 'y ~ x'
