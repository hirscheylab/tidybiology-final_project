Conclusions

From the plots shown above, we observe that some of the ZNF genes have tissue-specific expression (eg. ZNF529) where they are highly expressed in some tissues but not all. At the same time, there are some ZNF genes that are highly expressed in all tissue types studied (like ZNF274) while others are expressed at a low level in all tissues (like ZNF711). In all, ZNF genes that have tissue-specific expression may contribute to a specific function in those tissues whereas others may be broadly involved in tissue development and differentiation.

Prioritized follow-up studies

For future studies, we are interested in looking at genes that have tissue-specific expression (referred here as candidate genes). Conducting functional analysis (using knockout or knockdown experiments) would be useful to study the effects of these candidate genes on specific tissues. Furthermore, gene regulation studies would be instrumental in investigating the regulatory mechanisms behind tissue-specific gene expression, linking expression profiles to biological pathways.
