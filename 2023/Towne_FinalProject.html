TidyBiology Final Project: Identifying top gene promoters of brain metastasis

Anna Towne

2023-11-06

Three (3) RNA sequencing datasets have been identified which each compare the transcriptome of a parental cancer cell line and its brain metastatic (BrM) derivative. By combining these three individual comparisons across their three distinct cell lines, we may identify gene candidates which play a role in promoting brain metastasis (theoretically) regardless of primary tumor type.

Conclusions

Most genes appear to remain transcriptionally stable in brain metastatic cell lines as compared to parental lines with an average log2(FC) value near 0. However, some genes demonstrate conserved upregulation across imported datasets including collagen type VIII alpha 1 chain (COL8A1), atypical chemokine receptor 3 (ACKR3), and bone morphogenetic protein 2 (BMP2). The most downregulated genes include troponin C type 1 (TNNC1), insulin like growth factor binding protein 5 (IGFBP5), and cysteine rich protein 2 (CRIP2).

Future directions

Follow-up of this study would entail identifying upregulated gene hits which are both therapeutically targetable and remain largely unexplored in the context of brain metastasis. For example, while previous literature links ACKR3 to both metastasis and brain cancer, the role of ACKR3 in promoting brain metastasis may yet be understudied. Further, a potent, selective, [and] orally available ACKR3 antagonist was discovered in 2020 (Richard-Bildstein et al.), lending further investigation of the impact of ACKR3 inhibition on cell migration and cranial infiltration through combined in vitro and in vivo studies.
