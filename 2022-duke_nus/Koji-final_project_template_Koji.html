DESCRIBE YOUR DATA

ESCs produce all type of cells, and therefore, they must have extensive mechanisms to keep their stemness and genomic stability. Loss of the tumor suppressor p53 causes aneuploidy and genetic instability in ESCs, and p53 supports pluripotency, suggesting that p53 is important not only to prevent cancer but also for ESC homeostasis and quality. However, the underlying mechanisms are unknown. To identify p53 target genes that mediate these p53 functions, we knocked out p53 from human H9 ESCs by CRISPR/Cas9 system and did RNAseq analysis between parental and seven p53 knockout clones.

SET UP THE BIG FINALE

It is not very clear because the dots were condensed and overlapped in small places. Therefore, I changed to a logarithmic scale. I also changed the title and legend to improve the appearance. Rainbow colors are also used to make the changes in the log2 fold more obvious.

ggplot(ESC_p53, aes(WT1, KO1))+
    geom_point(aes(colour = fold1),
             size = 1.5,
             alpha = 0.7)+
  scale_color_gradientn(colours = rainbow(5))+
  scale_x_continuous(trans = "log10")+
  scale_y_continuous(trans = "log10")+

labs(x = "Parental (read count, log10)", 
       y = "p53 knockout (read count, log10)", 
       title = "RNAseq analysis between parental and p53 knockout H9 hESC", color = "Log2 fold change")+
theme(plot.title = element_text(face = "bold", size = 10),
     legend.background = element_rect(fill="gray", colour="black"),
     legend.position = "right", legend.title = element_text(size=10))

Conclusions

Although majority of gene expression was not changed by p53 knockout, I saw several genes were either induced or repressed by p53. p53 is known to induce and repress genes depending on gene types. However, this plot shows that there are more p53-inducible genes than p53-repressible genes. There are several genes induced by p53, ranging from almost zero to 5-30 fold or more, which is quite interesting.
