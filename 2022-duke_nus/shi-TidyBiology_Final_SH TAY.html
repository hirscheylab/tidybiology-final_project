TidyBiology score and kidney malignancies

It has been widely reported that a TidyBiology score of more than 2.0 strongly correlates with oncogenic potential. I wanted to identify novel proto-oncogenes in the kidney that may be implicated in early renal cell cancers, and which cell type(s) highly express those genes.

First, I looked at the distribution of scores of genes present in the kidney.

Genes with high TidyBiology scores in the kidney - what are they?

After filtering for genes whose scores exceed 2.0, I looked into their identities and corresponding scores.

Creating a TidyBiology Renal Malignancy Score

I then created a TidyBiology Renal Malignancy Score by summing the individual scores of every candidate gene for every cell. This will help me to evaluate, in the kidney, the cell types that have the highest oncogenic potential in patients with early renal cell cancers.

I first used a pivot table to reorganise the cell cluster columns so that I can obtain, per gene of interest, the number of reads for each cell cluster. I then summed up, per cell cluster, the reads for all genes to generate a score that measures malignancy potential.

renal_score <- tidybiology_score_2 %>% 
  pivot_longer(cols = cell_1:cell_10,  names_to = "Cluster", values_to = "value") %>% 
  group_by(Cluster)%>%
  summarise(Malignancy_score=sum(value))

This is a summary of the malignancy scores in descending order.

Conclusions

4 cell clusters have high TidyBiology Renal Malignancy scores: cell_6, cell_2, cell_7 and cell_8. In particular, cell_8 is of great interest as its role in driving early renal cell carcinoma, a type of renal malignancy, has been reported in murine studies.

The 4 genes with high TidyBiology scores are also of interest, as they are essential to renal physiology but may be subverted in malignancy.

Prioritized follow-up studies

I would study the 4 cell clusters with high TidyBiology Renal Malignancy scores - looking into their developmental trajectories and potential interactions with one another. I would then measure their levels in murine models of renal cancer with and without certain forms of treatment.
